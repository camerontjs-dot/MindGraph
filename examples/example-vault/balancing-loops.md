---
title: Balancing loops
domain: systems-thinking
---

A balancing loop is a feedback structure that pushes a system back toward a setpoint. A thermostat is the classic example: the room cools, the heater turns on, the room warms, the heater turns off. Predator-prey populations behave the same way at a longer time scale.

The visible signature of a balancing loop is oscillation around the setpoint with delay. The delay matters. A balancing loop with no delay produces smooth correction. A balancing loop with a long delay can swing wildly, overshoot, and look like instability before it settles.

Balancing loops paired with reinforcing loops produce most of the [[systems-archetypes]] (related) catalogued in the literature.
